About Jake

I'm a full-stack web developer currently in training at Northwestern University’s Coding Boot Camp. I spend most of my days of exploring HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I thrive on building things that help people — things that delight, inform, and, if I’m lucky, create memorable, meaningful experiences for those who use them. I’ve learned a great deal over the past year learning more about User Experience design, which has changed the way I look at the world, my code, and how people interact with just about everything. It has instilled a deep sense of empathy within me for those who use the things that I build and forces me to constantly answer the same question in new ways: “How could this be better?”

I’m curious and enjoy work that enables me to learn something new. As such, I try to stay on top of changes in my field so that I can be all the more prepared to meet any and all challenges with tools that are well suited to whatever may be at hand. I may be relatively new at this, but I’m a quick study and am eager to learn more.

Upon graduation in October 2017, I’ll be pursuing opportunities as a developer within an organization that will challenge, inspire, and allow me to continue to both grow and build upon my knowledge base. If you’re interested in my work or hiring me, it's wjthornberry everywhere: follow me on Twitter (@wjthornberry), fork me on GitHub, drop me an email, or see what's recently caught my eye on Instagram.

When I’m not exploring new ways to solve interesting problems, I love reading, distance running, photography, and exploring the city on my bike. I live in Chicago with my partner and dog.
